If you've lived in Indore for a few years, you've seen the evidence: white crusty deposits around tap bases, a geyser that takes longer and longer to heat, or reddish stains in the toilet bowl. All of these are symptoms of hard water — and Indore's groundwater is among the harder supplies in central India.

Understanding what hard water does to your plumbing helps you act before it becomes a costly problem.

## What Makes Indore's Water Hard?

"Hardness" refers to dissolved calcium and magnesium minerals in the water. Indore draws heavily from borewell sources that pass through calcium-rich limestone formations. Municipal supply in Indore (primarily from the Narmada-based supply via Jawahar Sagar and Yashwant Sagar) is treated and significantly softer — but many areas (Vijay Nagar, Nipania, Bhawarkuan, and eastern Indore localities) supplement with borewell water, especially during supply shortages.

Typical hardness levels measured in Indore areas:
- Municipal supply: 150–250 mg/L (moderately hard)
- Mixed municipal + borewell: 300–500 mg/L (hard)
- Pure borewell: 500–800 mg/L (very hard)

The WHO guideline for acceptable drinking water hardness is 200 mg/L. Much of Indore's supply exceeds this.

## How Hard Water Damages Your Plumbing — Component by Component

### Geysers (Water Heaters)

This is where hard water causes the most damage, fastest. When water is heated, calcium carbonate precipitates out of solution and sticks to the heating element and the inner tank wall. This limescale layer:

- **Insulates the element** from water, so the element has to get hotter to heat the same water — increasing electricity consumption by 15–30%
- **Overheats the element**, reducing its lifespan from 8–10 years to 3–5 years
- **Corrodes the tank** from inside if the sacrificial anode rod isn't replaced annually

Signs your geyser is suffering from hard water: takes longer to heat, popping or rumbling sounds during heating, discoloured (slightly rusty or chalky) hot water.

**Fix:** Annual descaling service + anode rod check. If the element has already failed, PS Tech can replace it same-day for most geyser models. See [our geyser repair service](/services).

### Taps and Faucets

Limescale builds up inside tap aerators (the mesh screen at the tip), reducing flow to a trickle. It also gets under washers and O-rings, preventing a proper seal — causing the tap to drip even when new.

External deposits around the base of taps are cosmetic but indicate heavy scaling inside too.

**Fix:** Clean aerators monthly by soaking in white vinegar overnight. For internal scaling affecting the valve mechanism, tap replacement is usually more cost-effective than continued repair.

### Pipes — The Slow Build

CPVC and UPVC pipes (now standard in new builds) are not significantly affected by limescale — the smooth inner surface doesn't allow scale to stick easily. However, **old GI (galvanised iron) pipes** in Indore's pre-2005 housing stock are highly vulnerable. The corrosion products from GI pipes actually combine with limescale deposits to form a hard, restrictive scale that can reduce pipe bore by 50–60% over 15–20 years.

The result: brown water when taps are first opened, falling pressure throughout the house, and eventually pinhole leaks.

**Fix:** GI to CPVC/UPVC pipe replacement. PS Tech has done this across [Mahalaxmi Nagar](/service-areas), older Palasia housing, and similar areas. It's a one-time investment that eliminates years of repeated repairs.

### Shower Heads

Mineral deposits block shower nozzles, turning a powerful even spray into an irregular dribble. This is the most visible and most easily addressed hard-water problem.

**Fix:** Soak the shower head in white vinegar for 4–8 hours and use a pin to clear blocked nozzles. Replacement shower heads with silicone nozzles (which resist scale) are a better long-term solution.

## Long-Term Solutions for Hard Water in Indore

1. **Water softener:** An ion-exchange unit that replaces calcium/magnesium with sodium. Effective but requires regular salt refilling and maintenance.
2. **Scale inhibitor:** An electronic or magnetic device fitted to the main inlet pipe. These reduce (but don't eliminate) scale formation and are maintenance-free.
3. **RO + UV system:** A reverse osmosis system removes hardness minerals completely from drinking water. A combination of whole-house softening (for plumbing) and RO (for drinking) is the gold standard.
4. **Annual geyser servicing:** The most cost-effective single action — descale the element, replace the anode rod, and check all seals.
