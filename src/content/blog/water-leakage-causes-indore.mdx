Water leakage in an Indore home is rarely a one-off accident. It's almost always the result of a cumulative problem — pipes under stress, joints weakened by hard water, or fixtures past their service life. The frustrating part is that most leaks are hidden behind walls or under floors for months before they cause visible damage.

Here are the 7 most common causes we encounter as plumbers in Indore — and what you should do about each.

## 1. Hard Water Corrosion (Indore's Biggest Culprit)

Indore's groundwater is hard — typically 300–600 mg/L TDS in borewell-fed areas like Vijay Nagar and Nipania. This mineral-heavy water deposits calcium and magnesium inside pipes, gradually narrowing the internal diameter and creating pressure hotspots. Over time, this accelerates corrosion — especially in older galvanised iron (GI) pipes.

**Signs:** Crusty white deposits around tap bases, reduced flow from all taps simultaneously, discoloured water.

**Fix:** If your home has GI pipes older than 15 years, it's time to replace them with CPVC or UPVC. This is a one-time investment that eliminates recurring hard-water damage. We do these replacements regularly in Mahalaxmi Nagar and older Indore colonies. Read our guide on [hard water damage to Indore plumbing](/blog/hard-water-indore).

## 2. Loose or Worn Pipe Joints

Every bend, T-junction, and coupling in your plumbing is a potential leak point. Over years of thermal expansion (hot summer days in Indore cause plastic pipes to expand and contract), threaded joints loosen and rubber gaskets dry out.

**Signs:** Damp patches on walls near junctions, musty smell in walls, slightly higher water bills.

**Fix:** A plumber can pressure-test your system to find which joint is leaking, then tighten, re-tape with PTFE, or replace the fitting — often a 30-minute job.

## 3. High Water Pressure

Many homes in Indore receive municipal supply at high pressure during early morning peak hours. This surge stresses every fixture in the system — especially older taps and toilet fill valves.

**Signs:** Banging pipes (water hammer), taps dripping within months of fitting, shower heads leaking at the base.

**Fix:** Installing a pressure reduction valve (PRV) at the municipal supply inlet. This protects your entire plumbing system and extends the life of all fittings significantly.

## 4. Faulty Tap Washers and O-rings

A dripping tap is the most visible and most ignored form of water leakage. A single dripping tap wastes 15–20 litres per day. In a 3-bedroom Indore flat with 4–5 taps, that adds up fast.

**Signs:** Visible drips from tap spout even when fully closed, water around the tap base.

**Fix:** Replacing the rubber washer and O-ring inside the tap — a 15-minute job. If the tap seat is also damaged (common in hard-water areas), a seat grinding tool or new tap may be needed.

## 5. Toilet Tank Leaks

Toilets are often the biggest hidden water waster in Indian homes. The fill valve and flapper inside the cistern wear out every 3–5 years — and when they do, water silently flows from the tank to the bowl 24×7.

**Signs:** Trickling sound from the toilet even when not recently flushed. Drop red food colouring in the tank — if colour appears in the bowl without flushing, you have a leak.

**Fix:** Replace the flapper valve and/or float assembly. We carry parts for all common Indian cistern brands and can usually fix this same-day.

## 6. Monsoon Soil Movement and Root Intrusion

Indore's intense June–August monsoon (typically 900–1,100 mm rainfall) causes soil movement and waterlogging that put stress on underground drain pipes. Tree roots near drainage lines can also infiltrate through hairline cracks in older concrete pipes.

**Signs:** Drain backflow or slow drainage after heavy rain, unusual puddles in the garden, sewage smell.

**Fix:** CCTV drain inspection (we have this capability) to identify root intrusion or pipe collapse, followed by targeted jetting or sectional pipe replacement. Sump pump installation for monsoon protection in basement areas — see our guide on [sump pumps and monsoon waterlogging](/blog/sump-pump-monsoon-indore).

## 7. Aging Geyser Tank Corrosion

A water heater (geyser) holds pressurised hot water — the worst environment for a tank. Without annual servicing (draining sediment, replacing the sacrificial anode rod), the tank corrodes from the inside out. When it fails, you get a sudden leak from the unit — sometimes significant.

**Signs:** Rust-coloured hot water, pooling water under the geyser, popping or rumbling sounds when heating.

**Fix:** Annual geyser servicing extends tank life. If the tank wall has already corroded, replacement is usually more cost-effective than repair. [Read our full geyser troubleshooting guide](/blog/geyser-not-heating-indore).

## When to Call a Plumber Immediately

Most leaks worsen quickly. Call PS Tech right away if you notice:
- Water bubbling up from the floor or ceiling
- A sudden drop in water pressure across the whole house
- Visible mould or damp patches on interior walls
- Your water meter moving when all taps are off
