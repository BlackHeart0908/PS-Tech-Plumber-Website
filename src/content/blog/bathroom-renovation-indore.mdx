A bathroom renovation in Indore involves more decisions than most homeowners anticipate — and most of the costly mistakes happen when the sequence of work gets reversed (tiles laid before plumbing is tested, waterproofing skipped, drain positions fixed before shower choice is confirmed). 

This checklist puts everything in the right order.

## Phase 1: Planning (Before Anyone Touches the Bathroom)

### Decide Your Layout First

Changing the position of the toilet, shower drain, or wash basin during renovation dramatically increases cost because it means breaking more floor/wall and re-routing drain pipes. Decide your layout before any work starts.

Questions to answer upfront:
- Indian or Western toilet? If switching, the drain position changes significantly.
- Shower only, or shower + bathtub?
- Is the existing wash basin position working, or will it shift?
- Where will the geyser go? Inside or outside the bathroom?

### Fix Your Budget Realistically

Bathroom renovation costs in Indore (2024 approximate ranges):
- Basic refresh (taps, fittings, painting): ₹15,000–₹40,000
- Mid-range renovation (new tiles, new fittings, concealed cistern, shower): ₹60,000–₹1.5 lakh
- Premium renovation (premium tiles, rain shower, bathtub, imported fittings): ₹2–5 lakh+

Material cost (especially tiles and sanitary ware) is typically 50–60% of total budget. Get 2–3 quotes from contractors including labour + material.

## Phase 2: Plumbing Rough Work (Do This First)

This is the critical phase that determines everything else. **Do not tile before the plumbing rough work is tested.**

### What Plumbing Rough Work Involves
1. **Breaking existing walls/floor** to access concealed pipes
2. **Re-routing supply pipes** to new tap/shower/geyser positions
3. **Shifting drain points** if layout is changing
4. **Installing concealed cistern box** in the wall (if using wall-hung or concealed flush toilet)
5. **Geyser supply line** — hot and cold water supply to shower, basin, and any bathtub

### Waterproofing — The Most-Skipped Step

After rough plumbing and before tiling, the floor and at least 1.5 metres up all walls must be waterproofed. In Indore, where ground floors often experience monsoon moisture and bathrooms in upper floors share walls with bedrooms, skipping waterproofing causes permanent water damage to adjacent rooms within 2–3 years.

**Use:** Cementitious waterproofing (Fosroc, STP Roff, or Dr. Fixit) applied in 2 coats. Let it cure fully (48 hours minimum) before tiling.

### Test All Rough Plumbing Before Tiling

Before the tiler starts, run water through all supply lines and check:
- No drips at any joint
- Drain flows freely and doesn't back up
- Hot water reaches the shower point correctly

## Phase 3: Tiling

Once waterproofing is confirmed and dry:
- Set drain cover level — tiles must slope towards drain (1:100 gradient for bathroom floors)
- Plan tile layout from the centre of the room outward to avoid small cuts at visible edges
- Use epoxy grout in bathrooms (not regular cement grout) — it's mould-resistant

## Phase 4: Sanitary Fittings & Final Plumbing

This is done after tiling:
1. Install toilet (connect supply, connect drain, test flush)
2. Install wash basin and taps
3. Install shower head and mixer
4. Connect geyser (or fit a new one) — [see geyser installation guide](/services)
5. Install accessories: towel rails, soap dish, toilet paper holder (these should be fixed to wall plugs in tile joints, not cutting through tiles)

## Phase 5: Ventilation, Lighting & Electrical

- Exhaust fan installation (ensure it's rated for wet areas)
- Waterproof IP65-rated lights in shower zone
- ELCB (Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker) must be fitted for all bathroom electrical points
