A drain that clogs once is a nuisance. A drain that clogs every 2–4 weeks is a symptom — and treating only the symptom (pouring drain cleaner or calling a plumber for a quick clear) without finding the cause wastes time and money.

Here's how to diagnose why your drain keeps blocking, and what the lasting fix is.

## The Five Real Causes of Recurring Drain Blockages

### 1. Grease Buildup in Kitchen Drains

Kitchen grease — cooking oil, ghee, butter — goes down the drain as a liquid but solidifies as it cools in the pipe. Over weeks, a sticky grease layer builds on the pipe wall, trapping food particles and reducing flow until it blocks completely.

This is the most common cause of kitchen drain blockages in Indore homes and is entirely preventable.

**Prevention:**
- Never pour cooking oil or grease down the drain
- Pour used cooking oil into a container and dispose in the bin
- Once a week, flush the kitchen drain with a kettle of boiling water (for metal pipes) or very hot tap water (for PVC pipes) — this melts and flushes the grease before it can solidify into a thick layer

**Fix:** For an established grease blockage, a plumber will use a motorised drain snake or pressure jetter. Chemical drain cleaners dissolve grease slowly but aren't effective for heavy buildup.

### 2. Hair in Bathroom Drains

This is the primary cause of bathroom shower and basin drain blocks. A ball of hair tangled with soap scum creates a near-impenetrable plug that gets worse with every shower.

**Prevention:** A simple stainless steel drain strainer (₹50–150) catches almost all hair before it enters the drain. Clean it after every shower. This single habit prevents 90% of bathroom drain blockages.

**Fix:** A drain snake (or a straightened wire hanger) to pull the hair plug out manually. No chemical needed. For persistent build-up deeper in the pipe, call a plumber for professional clearing.

### 3. Insufficient Drain Fall (Poor Original Plumbing)

Every horizontal drain pipe must slope downward toward the stack — at least 1 cm per 40 cm of pipe (1:40 gradient). Without this fall, waste doesn't flow by gravity; solids settle and accumulate. This is a common issue in older Indore buildings and in any home where plumbing was done without proper supervision.

**Signs of insufficient fall:** Drain runs slowly even when newly cleaned. Toilet drainage is sluggish even with a full flush. Kitchen sink gurgles when the bath drains (shared stack issues).

**Fix:** This requires re-routing the drain pipe to restore the correct gradient. It's a proper plumbing job — not a DIY fix — but it's a permanent solution versus endless clearing.

### 4. Root Intrusion in Underground Drains

Trees in your garden or on the street can send roots into underground drain pipes through tiny cracks and joints. Roots don't just block — they spread and crack the pipe over months, making the problem progressively worse.

**Signs:** Slow drainage across multiple points in the house simultaneously. Drain backing up after rain. Faint sewage smell in the garden.

**Fix:** High-pressure water jetting clears root mass, but if the pipe is cracked, sectional pipe replacement is needed. CCTV drain camera inspection (PS Tech can arrange this for persistent cases) is the definitive diagnostic.

### 5. Blocked Vent Pipe

Drain pipes need air to flow properly. Every drain system has a vertical vent pipe that runs up through the roof. If this vent is blocked (by a birds' nest, leaves, or debris), the drain system loses its air supply — water drains slowly and makes gurgling sounds because it's trying to pull air back through the drain itself.

**Signs:** All drains in the house are sluggish simultaneously. Gurgling sound from floor drains or toilets when another drain is used.

**Fix:** Clearing the vent pipe from the roof level. A plumber or general builder can snake a wire down the vent stack to clear the obstruction.

## When to Use Chemical Drain Cleaners (and When Not To)

**Use:** For grease or hair blockages in PVC drain pipes, an alkaline drain cleaner (caustic soda / sodium hydroxide-based) or enzymatic cleaner can help as a maintenance measure once a month.

**Don't use:**
- On complete blockages (it doesn't work fast enough and may splash back)
- On pipes that connect to a septic tank (kills the beneficial bacteria)
- On very old PVC pipes with joints — the chemical can soften PVC over time
- As a substitute for finding the actual cause
