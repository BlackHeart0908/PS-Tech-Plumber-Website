Electrical problems in Indian homes are quietly serious. The Bureau of Energy Efficiency estimates that electrical faults account for over 25% of residential fires in India — and Indore, with its mix of older wiring in established colonies and rapidly growing new construction, is no exception.

The good news: most electrical hazards give warning signs before they become emergencies. Here's how to read them.

## Warning Signs of a Dangerous Electrical System

### 1. Frequently Tripping MCBs

Your MCB (Miniature Circuit Breaker) is doing its job when it trips — it's protecting your wiring from overload. But if a circuit trips regularly (more than once a week), it means one of three things:

- **Overloaded circuit:** Too many appliances on one circuit. Solution: redistribute load or add a dedicated circuit for the high-draw appliance (e.g., AC, geyser, washing machine).
- **Faulty appliance:** The appliance on that circuit is drawing excessive current. Test by plugging the appliance into a different circuit.
- **Wiring fault:** A short circuit or insulation failure somewhere in the wiring. **Call an electrician.** Don't just keep resetting the MCB.

### 2. Burning Smell or Discolouration at Switch/Socket

A burning plastic smell near a switchboard, or yellowing/scorching marks around a socket, means arcing is occurring — electricity is jumping through a gap caused by a loose connection or degraded insulation. This is a fire risk.

**Action:** Switch off the MCB for that circuit immediately. Do not use that socket or switch until an electrician has inspected and replaced the wiring and fittings.

### 3. Lights Flickering or Dimming

Occasional flickering during thunderstorms (voltage fluctuations from the supply) is normal. But constant flickering or lights dimming when you turn on a large appliance indicates either:

- A loose connection at the main distribution board or in the wiring
- Undersized wire for the connected load
- A failing neutral connection

All three require an electrician to investigate.

### 4. Electric Shock from Appliances or Fixtures

A mild tingle when touching an appliance, tap, or even a wall is a classic symptom of a failed earthing (grounding) system. In India, earthing is mandatory but poorly maintained in many older properties. Without proper earth, fault current from a leaking appliance has nowhere safe to go — and uses you as its path to ground.

**Action:** Immediately switch off and unplug the appliance. Call an electrician to check the earthing continuity across your home. This is a genuine safety issue, not something to defer.

### 5. Very Old Wiring (Pre-2000 Indore Construction)

Homes built before approximately 2000 in Indore often have aluminium wiring (problematic — prone to loose joints and corrosion) or severely degraded PVC insulation that has become brittle and cracked with age. The wiring capacity also predates modern appliance loads — a home wired for a 1kW load in 1990 may now run a 3kW geyser, 1.5T AC, and a washing machine simultaneously.

If your Indore home is more than 20 years old and hasn't been rewired, a professional inspection is worthwhile. PS Tech's electrical team can assess your switchboard, wiring condition, earthing, and load capacity.

## Which Electrical Jobs Require a Licensed Electrician?

### Always use a licensed electrician for:
- **Main distribution board work** — adding or upgrading MCBs, adding circuits
- **ELCB/RCCB installation** — earth leakage protection, mandatory for wet areas
- **Wiring a new room** — running cables inside walls and conduits
- **Earthing installation or testing** — critical safety work
- **Any work near the main incoming supply** — including the energy meter
- **Installing high-load appliances** — geysers, ACs, EV chargers need dedicated circuits

### Minor jobs you can do yourself safely:
- Replacing a plug on an appliance cord (with the power switched off)
- Replacing a light bulb (LED swap, with power off at the switch)
- Resetting a tripped MCB (once, to test if it holds — if it trips again, call an electrician)

### Never attempt yourself:
- Opening the main distribution board
- Splicing or joining concealed wiring
- Installing or replacing switchboard outlets without isolating the circuit
- Any work near the utility service entry point

## Electrical Safety Before Monsoon in Indore

Indore's monsoon season (June–August) is the highest-risk period for electrical accidents in residential areas because:

- Moisture enters switchboards through wall cracks
- Earthing systems deteriorate faster in waterlogged soil
- Power fluctuations and outages are frequent, leading to repeated switching and potential arcing

**Pre-monsoon electrical checklist:**
- Test all ELCB/RCCB units (press the test button — they should trip)
- Check for any exposed wiring near windows, balconies, or external walls
- Ensure all outdoor sockets have weatherproof covers
- Verify the geyser's earthing is intact (tingle when touching? Call us immediately)
