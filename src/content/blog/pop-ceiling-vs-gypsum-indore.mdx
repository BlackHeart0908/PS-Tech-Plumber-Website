If you're renovating or building in Indore and considering a false ceiling, you'll quickly run into the POP vs. gypsum question. Both are excellent materials, both are widely available in Indore, and both are installed by PS Tech's ceiling team — but they suit different rooms, budgets, and design goals.

Here's the honest comparison.

## What Is POP (Plaster of Paris)?

POP is calcium sulfate hemihydrate — a fine white powder that mixes with water to form a workable paste that hardens on drying. In ceiling work, it's applied wet by skilled plasterers to create smooth surfaces, curved profiles, cornices, and intricate decorative elements.

**Think of POP as:** Handcrafted, custom, sculptural. Every design is made in-situ by a craftsman.

## What Is Gypsum Board (Drywall)?

Gypsum board (also called plasterboard or drywall) is a factory-manufactured panel: a gypsum core pressed between two layers of paper facing. It's installed in a grid of metal channels, then joints are taped and filled for a smooth finish.

**Think of gypsum as:** Factory precision, fast installation, consistent finish. Ideal for clean-lined modern interiors.

## POP vs. Gypsum — The Key Comparisons

### Design Flexibility

**POP wins hands-down for complex designs.** Curved surfaces, domes, arches, sculptural light coves, decorative borders, and intricate cornices can only be achieved easily with POP. This is why POP remains the dominant ceiling material for traditional and ornate interior styles, which are still very popular in Indore homes.

**Gypsum is better for clean geometric designs** — flat ceilings with recessed lighting, tray ceilings (step-downs), and box coves. It can be curved, but requires additional effort.

### Installation Time

| Aspect | POP | Gypsum Board |
|---|---|---|
| Typical 200 sq ft ceiling | 3–5 days | 1–2 days |
| Drying/curing time | 48+ hours needed | Minimal |
| Readiness for painting | After full cure | Sooner |

Gypsum is significantly faster, which matters if you're renovating a room you need to use quickly.

### Cost (Indore Market, 2024)

**POP:** ₹45–90 per sq ft, depending on design complexity (simple flat ceiling on the lower end, intricate cornice + cove on the higher end).

**Gypsum:** ₹60–120 per sq ft (board + frame + finishing). Simple flat gypsum ceiling is roughly comparable to POP; commercial grid ceiling is cheaper.

For simple flat ceilings in a bedroom, costs are similar. For complex decorative work, POP typically costs less because gypsum needs additional fabrication.

### Moisture Resistance

This is a critical consideration for Indore's climate.

**Standard gypsum board:** Not moisture-resistant. In bathrooms, kitchens, or areas prone to water leaks, standard gypsum ceiling absorbs moisture and collapses. Never install standard gypsum in a bathroom ceiling.

**Moisture-resistant (MR) gypsum:** Available and suitable for kitchens and bathrooms. Slightly higher cost.

**POP:** More tolerant of incidental moisture but not waterproof. Will develop cracks if there's a sustained water leak above it.

For bathrooms: use either MR gypsum or POP with proper waterproofing above.
For air-conditioned rooms: gypsum is preferred, as condensation drips from AC units don't affect it as much as POP.

### Crack Resistance

POP ceilings are prone to hairline cracks at joints and corners, especially in the first year as the building settles. These are easily patched, but can be annoying if you've just painted.

Gypsum board with properly taped joints is much less prone to cracking. This is why gypsum is preferred in commercial settings and in newer high-rise apartments in Vijay Nagar and Nipania.

### Weight and Structural Load

Gypsum board ceiling: ~12–14 kg per sq metre.
POP ceiling (thick): ~18–25 kg per sq metre.

For older Indore buildings with thinner roof slabs, POP's extra weight is something to discuss with your contractor.
